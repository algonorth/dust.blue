Ten Billion Years

The life of a star, compressed into one scroll.

SCROLL THROUGH DEEP TIME

CH. 01 / T MINUS ONE MILLION YEARS

It begins with
almost nothing.

A cloud of cold hydrogen, ten degrees above absolute zero, wider than four hundred solar systems laid end to end. Too thin to see. Too heavy to survive itself.

It has been waiting here since before the Sun, for a shove, a shockwave, an excuse.

CH. 02 / THE COLLAPSE

Gravity
always wins.

A dying star detonates somewhere nearby. The blast leans on the cloud, and the cloud falls. A hundred thousand years of falling, spinning faster as it shrinks, flattening into a disk with a furnace-hot heart.

YOUR CURSOR IS GRAVITY NOW. DRAG IT THROUGH THE DUST

CH. 03 / T = 0

At fifteen million degrees,
hydrogen forgets
it was ever alone.

Fusion. A star is born, and begins to shine against its own collapse.

CH. 04 / THE MAIN SEQUENCE

Then, for ten billion years,
nothing happens.

This is the long adulthood. Six hundred million tons of hydrogen fused every second, and the star doesn't change for longer than the age of the universe. Planets condense out of the leftovers and settle into their lanes.

YOUR CURSOR IS RADIATION PRESSURE NOW. IT PUSHES

CH. 05 / THE RED GIANT

The core runs dry.
The star survives by
burning its own ash.

Hydrogen spent, the heart contracts and ignites its helium ash. The outer layers swell two hundred times over, swallowing the near planets whole and boiling the far ones.

CH. 06 / THE SHEDDING

A star this size
does not explode.
It exhales.

Pulse by pulse, the star lets go of everything it spent ten billion years holding: carbon, oxygen, nitrogen, blown into glowing veils a light-year wide.

CH. 07 / THE WHITE DWARF

What remains:
an ember the size of Earth,
a spoonful weighs a ton.

No fusion. No fuel. Just leftover heat radiating into the dark, a cooling that will outlast every star now shining.

CH. 08 / ELSEWHERE, THE OTHER ENDING

A star ten times heavier
does not exhale.
It falls forever.

Its core collapses through every floor physics can build: past iron, past neutron, past light itself. What remains is a hole in the story, dressed in the blazing breath of everything it is still swallowing.

THE ARCS ABOVE AND BELOW ARE THE DISK'S FAR SIDE. GRAVITY BENDS IT INTO VIEW

EPILOGUE / T PLUS FOREVER

You were forged,
atom by atom, in a furnace
exactly like this one.

The iron in your blood. The oxygen in your first breath. A star gave them up so you could borrow them for a while.
